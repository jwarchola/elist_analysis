# [made up company name placeholder] Electronics E-Commerce Performance Analysis

Sales trends analysis for an e-commerce company

<h1 align="left">Background</h1>

Bob’s Electronics is a US-based e-commerce company that sells popular consumer electronics and accessories to a global customer base. Founded in 2018, the company experienced significant growth and expansion, but is now facing challenges brought on by the COVID-19 pandemic.

Bob’s Electronics services almost 88,000 customers in 194 countries and possesses over 108,000 transactions, generating sales revenue exceeding $28 million.

Reporting to the Head of Operations, an in-depth analysis was conducted to evaluate Bob’s Electronics performance over the four year period (2019–2022). This comprehensive review provides valuable insights that internal cross-functional teams will utilize to streamline processes and enhance Bob’s Electronics commercial performance. The key insights and recommendations focus on the following areas:

**Northstar Metrics**

- Sales Trends - Focusing on key metrics of sales revenue, number of orders placed, and average order value (AOV).
- Product Performance - Analyzing different product lines, market impact, and refund rates to inform strategic product decisions.
- Loyalty Program Evaluation - Evaluating the effectiveness of the company's loyalty program and providing recommendations to maximize customer engagement and retention.
- Regional Results - Evaluating regional demand and product performance within regions to identify areas for improvement.

<h3 align="center"> Sales revenue peaked in 2021 followed by a decline starting in 2021</h3>

            <strong>Peak Sales Performance in 2020</strong>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Revenue grew by ~50% in March of 2020</strong>, marking the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, and continued to grow every quarter in 2020 due to the lockdown.</li>
              <li>The company experienced the highest sales revenue in 2020 totaling over $10.1M, with December 2020 being the best performing month on record at $1.25M in revenue</li>

            </ul>
          </li>
          <li>
            <strong>Downward Trend in 2022</strong>
            <ul>
              <li>A sharp decline in sales revenue can be seen in 2022, specifically in the months of October ($178K), November ($208k), and December ($262k) which are the lowest performing months on record</li>
              <li>Total sales revenue declined by ~46% in 2022 from 2021, however, total revenue in 2022 ($4.9M) is ~28% higher than the pre-pandemic revenue in 2019 ($3.9M)</li>
              <li>The decline in sales is most likely due to normalization after the pandemic</li>
            </ul>
          </li>
        </ol>
      </td>

        <ol start="4">
          <li>
            <strong>Q3 and Q4 drive sales revenue year to year</strong>
            <ul>
              <li>From 2019-2021, a trend of increased sales revenue during Q4 is observed, most likely driven by the holiday shopping season and the promotional days during that period (Black Friday, Cyber Monday, etc.)</li>
              <li>We can also see upticks in sales revenue during Q3, likely caused by the back-to-school season where students and parents are purchasing new devices. </li>
            </ul>
          </li>
          <li>
            <strong>Key Takeaways & Recommendations</strong>
            <ul>
              <li>Investigate the causes of the 2022 decline (e.g., market changes, competition, internal factors).</li>
              <li>Leverage high-performing periods (e.g., Q3 and Q4 of strong years) to refine marketing and sales strategies.</li>
              <li>Reassess business strategy for 2023, focusing on pricing, promotions, and customer engagement to regain momentum.</li>
            </ul>
          </li>
        </ol>
